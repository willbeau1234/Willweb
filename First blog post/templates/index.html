How It Works

1. Detect Where You Pause

We use phase correlation to track scroll movement frame-by-frame. When the screen stops moving for 0.5+ seconds, that's a "dwell" — you paused to look at something.

2. Score Each Post

An AI vision model scores each post you paused on across 5 dimensions: Appearance (beauty/body focus), Idealization (polished vs raw), Arousal (emotional intensity), Negativity (negative tone), and Aspiration (lifestyle envy).

3. Weight by Attention

ω = log(1 + dwell_time/τ) × (1 + γ×arousal) × (1 + δ×is_video)

Posts you looked at longer count more. High-arousal and video content get a boost (they're designed to capture attention).

4. Compute Distortion Score

Score = 100 × (α×weighted_mean + β×peak + (1-α-β)×end)

Combines your overall feed intensity with peak moments and how you ended (the "peak-end rule" from psychology — we remember peaks and endings most).

5. The Gap

"Stopped On" vs "Served" shows the difference between what you actually looked at vs what was just scrolled past. This reveals your personal engagement pattern.
